🎓 Daily Language School · Monday Theme: Work
🇩🇪 Su learns German
Feierabend
[FY-er-ah-bent]
The sacred German concept of "end of workday" — clocking off and switching fully off. No direct English translation.
"Feierabend!" = "That's it for today, I'm done."
"Ich mache um 18 Uhr Feierabend." = "I'm clocking off at 6pm."
🧠 Memory trick: "Feier" = celebration, "Abend" = evening. Germans celebrate every evening by actually stopping work. Revolutionary concept.
Bonus phrase: "Na, hast du schon Feierabend?" = "Done for the day yet?" — what Alex's family says when anyone leaves the Küche.
🇧🇷 Alex learns Portuguese
Expediente
[ex-pe-di-EN-tchi]
Business hours / the working day. What Su navigates every single Monday with more energy than most people manage on a Friday.
"O expediente termina às 18h." = "Business hours end at 6pm."
"Estou em pleno expediente." = "I'm in the middle of the work day."
🧠 Memory trick: Think "expedition" — every workday is a mission. Also sounds like "expedite," which is what Su does to every deal she touches.
Bonus: "Fora do expediente" = "outside of business hours" — the Brazilian art of not answering Slack after 6pm. Learn it.
