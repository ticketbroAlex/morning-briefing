🎓 Language School · Saturday — Leisure Edition
Su learns 🇩🇪 German
Ausschlafen
/ OWS-shlah-fen /
To sleep until completely rested — sleeping in without guilt or alarm
Am Samstag darf man endlich ausschlafen.
On Saturdays, you're finally allowed to properly sleep in.
🧠 Memory trick: "AUS" = out + "schlafen" = sleep → you sleep yourself completely OUT of exhaustion. Like fully draining a battery. Germans turned this into one beautiful, guilt-free word.
Bonus phrase: "Lass uns ausschlafen" = "Let's sleep in" — possibly the most romantic thing you can say to someone on a Saturday morning in Germany. 🛌

Alex learns 🇧🇷 Portuguese
Moleza
/ moh-LEH-zah /
That deliberate, delicious slowness — the joy of doing nothing important on purpose
Hoje é sábado, é dia de moleza total.
Today is Saturday — it's a full take-it-easy day.
🧠 Memory trick: Think "molasses" — slow, thick, sweet, going nowhere fast. Moleza is the Brazilian philosophy that sometimes the most productive thing you can do is absolutely nothing.
Bonus tip: Brazilians say "que moleza!" both as a complaint (ugh, so lazy!) and as a compliment (what a perfect lazy moment!). Context decides everything. 😌

💑 Couple Tip · Saturday — Presence
The Phone-Free Hour
High-performance couples don't fail at presence because they don't care — they fail because the default state is always device-in-hand. Saturday mornings are the highest-leverage moment to reset that default.

Pick one hour this morning — coffee, breakfast, a walk — where both phones go face-down in another room. No exceptions. No "just checking one thing." The rule has to be binary to hold. What you'll notice: within 15 minutes, you'll actually start talking about something real. Something that doesn't fit in a WhatsApp message. That's the payoff.

Micro-action: Agree on the hour right now, before either of you picks up a phone. Lock it in verbally. Accountability is the only mechanism that makes this work.
Name your phone-free hour together — and pick one real topic you both want to catch up on.
